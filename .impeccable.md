## Design Context

### Users
Public Pleros visitors who are exploring the Gospel, asking spiritual questions, trying to understand God's purpose, or deciding whether to take a deeper discipleship step through PPC.

### Brand Personality
Clear, warm, faith-rooted. The interface should feel invitational, grounded in Scripture, and steady rather than corporate or dashboard-heavy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Match the existing public Pleros website language: bright sky and brand-blue framing, soft pathway color surfaces, Sen-led display typography, rounded mobile-first sections, and restrained motion. Public pages should feel like part of the homepage, Questions, Purpose, About, Partner, Contact, and Vision and Mission pages. Avoid PPC admin styling and avoid stock shadcn presentation.

### Design Principles
1. Start from the current public shell, typography classes, spacing rhythm, and pathway colors before adding anything new.
2. Keep pages mobile-first with strong section structure and simple, obvious next steps.
3. Use sentence-case copy and warm, direct language that moves visitors toward clarity and discipleship.
4. Keep motion subtle and functional, with hover and transition details staying restrained.
5. Reuse public assets, content patterns, and components instead of inventing parallel systems.
